Definitions:

“Disguise” means to change the way you look or act so that people won’t recognize you. It’s like playing dress-up or pretending to be someone else to hide who you really are. For example, if you wear a mask or a hat to a party so your friends don’t recognize you at first, that’s a disguise.

  1. (verb) to alter one’s appearance or behavior in order to conceal one’s identity or to deceive someone.

    Example: The spy disguised herself as a tourist to avoid detection.

  2. (verb) to give a different appearance or impression of something, usually in order to make it more attractive or acceptable.

    Example: The marketing team disguised the bitter taste of the medicine with a sweet flavor.

  3. (noun) a false appearance or behavior that is intended to deceive someone.

    Example: The robber wore a disguise to conceal his identity.

Idioms & Phrases:

  1. in disguise: not in one’s true appearance or identity.

    Example: The superhero often walks around in disguise to avoid being recognized.

  2. disguise one’s feelings: to hide one’s emotions or thoughts.

    Example: Sarah disguised her disappointment with a smile when she didn’t get the job.

  3. wear a disguise: to wear a costume or a mask to change one’s appearance.

    Example: The spy wore a disguise to blend in with the crowd and avoid detection.

  4. in plain disguise: to be obvious or easy to recognize despite attempting to conceal one’s identity.

    Example: The celebrity tried to go incognito at the airport, but her bright pink hat was a dead giveaway in plain disguise.

  5. disguised as: to pretend to be something or someone else in order to deceive or avoid detection.

    Example: The thief disguised himself as a maintenance worker to sneak into the building undetected.

  6. a blessing in disguise: something that seems bad or unfortunate at first, but later turns out to be good.

    Example: Losing my job was a blessing in disguise because it forced me to pursue my dream career.

Word Origins:

The word “disguise” comes from the Old French word “desguiser,” which means “to change one’s appearance” or “to hide.” The prefix “dis-” means “apart” or “away,” and the root word “guise” means “appearance” or “manner.”
